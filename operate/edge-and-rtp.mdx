House edge is the percentage the house keeps per bet on average. Return to player is the inverse: `RTP = 100% - edge`.

## Originals

All eight originals support the same five edge levels. The default is **2%**.

| Game | 1% | 2% | 3% | 4% | 8% |
|:--|:--|:--|:--|:--|:--|
| Plinko | 99% | 98% | 97% | 96% | 92% |
| Keno | 99% | 98% | 97% | 96% | 92% |
| Dice | 99% | 98% | 97% | 96% | 92% |
| Advanced Dice | 99% | 98% | 97% | 96% | 92% |
| Limbo | 99% | 98% | 97% | 96% | 92% |
| Mines | 99% | 98% | 97% | 96% | 92% |

## Table games

Table games use standard casino odds. They are not configurable.

| Game | Edge | RTP |
|:--|:--|:--|
| Roulette (European) | 2.7% | 97.3% |
| Roulette (American) | 5.3% | 94.7% |
| Baccarat (Banker) | ~1.06% | ~98.94% |
| Baccarat (Player) | ~1.24% | ~98.76% |
| Baccarat (Tie) | ~14.36% | ~85.64% |
| Blackjack (basic strategy) | ~0.5% | ~99.5% |

## How edge affects payouts

For dynamic-multiplier games (Dice, Advanced Dice, Limbo, Mines), the formula is direct:

```text
multiplier = (100 - edge) / win_chance
```

For fixed-paytable games (Plinko, Keno, Wheel, Diamonds), each edge level has a pre-calculated paytable where multipliers are scaled down proportionally.

### Example: Plinko, high risk, 16 rows

| Edge | RTP | Max multiplier |
|:--|:--|:--|
| 1% | 99% | 1,000x |
| 2% | 98% | ~980x |
| 3% | 97% | ~970x |
| 4% | 96% | ~960x |
| 8% | 92% | ~920x |

## How to choose

| Edge | Best for |
|:--|:--|
| 1–2% | Competitive RTP in markets with many instant-game providers. Trades revenue per bet for volume and retention. |
| 3–4% | A balanced starting point for new markets and mid-volume platforms. |
| 8% | Maximum revenue per bet. Best where session length is short. |

<Tip>
A 2–3% edge is a typical starting point.
</Tip>
